Hello! My name is Kimberly, and I am a graduate student in the Chemical & Biomolecular Engineering Department at the University of California, Berkeley.

## Bio

I was born and raised in a suburb just south of Atlanta, Georgia. As I grew up I became increasingly interested in new and emerging technology. I graduated high school in 2014 with a strong interest in STEM. That same year, I began my undergraduate career at the Georgia Institute of Technology majoring in Chemical & Biomolecular Engineering. As an undergraduate, I became interested in coding as it applies to science and engineering, which led to the addition of a minor in Scientific and Engineering Computing. In addition, during my time at Georgia Tech, I worked as both an undergraduate teaching assistant and a tutor. I worked on an undergraduate project with the Maldovan group, where I independently attempted to build a COMSOL model of a pipe-in-pipe reactor for the production of syn gas. I graduated in 2018 with Highest Honors, but with some uncertainty in my future.

I spent a year off to focus on thinking about my future. As I was applying to jobs, I found that I had a stronger desire to go back to school to do research. At the end, I decided to move across the country to pursue a PhD in Chemical Engineering at the University of California, Berkeley.
